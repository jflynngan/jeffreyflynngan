my most current artistic statement

tldr: I'm drawn towards authentic failure, wild experimentation, remixing, and making people laugh. Most of my art is rooted in performance, as I play with other mediums: painting, collage, puppetry, dance, stand-up, whatever seems like fun!

I've found that I'm constantly changing my mind about who I am. I often have revelations about myself, then forget them...then rediscover something or remember another thing. I’m drawn to making a lot of different things. I act. I write computer code. I paint. I'm trying to make sculptures like Bob Rauschenberg...I compile monthly analytic data. I dance. I am always learning and trying a great deal of things. Some things I'm quite skilled at; other things I do because I enjoy the trying e.g. playing the piano — I am not skilled at playing the piano but I've been practicing playing it and improvising with it and having fun. I’m trying to manifest the future I want to see in the world: more resource sharing for historically oppressed populations, and less pain because of one’s identity. Making art is a good way for me to activate people's empathy and form a community around an experience. I'm learning computer programming so I can make things that can help people, whether it's bringing them new information or assisting them with something. When I make art I am thinking about how to craft:

  • Comedy
  • Spectacle
  • Musicality

I’m really comfortable stealing from artists right now, cause I’m figuring out who I am. Of course attributing is important. Taylor Mac’s mantra “perfection is for assholes” is big for me right now.

Theatre director JoAne Akalaitis said that chaos is the source of drama, not conflict. I take that literally as I do a lot of things. I’m a theatre person (if you call yourself a theatre person does that mean you won’t be seen as a visual artist or a sound artist or anything else?). Further, I enjoy the elements Akalaitis identifies: the illogical, the disruptive, the disturbing, and the reckless. Visually, I like messes. I like the juxtaposition of things that surprisingly go together. I have always been intimidated by visual art. But I’m pushing myself to be uncomfortable and try things, and forget the standards I agreed to at some point.
